How our medical review works

We combine clinical expertise, coder review and analytics to produce audit-ready findings. Our process is designed to be transparent, defensible, and aligned with payer regulations and accreditation standards.

  1. Scope & Intake: Samples, claim extracts, and clinical records to define project scope and KPIs.
  2. Clinical Review: Nurse/physician reviewers evaluate medical necessity, documentation quality and coding alignment.
  3. Repricing & Recovery: Where appropriate, repricing models are run and recovery opportunities are pursued with payers.
  4. Reporting & Remediation: Actionable reports, policy recommendations and training to close gaps.

Long description to attract clients

Payers, providers and employers rely on careful clinical review to ensure that care delivered is appropriate and documented — and that payments reflect the services provided. SamITHealth brings clinical rigor and operational discipline to medical review engagements. Our reviewers include experienced RNs, coders and physicians who apply evidence-based criteria and payer policies to each case. We produce defensible reviews that stand up to internal audits, external appeals and regulatory scrutiny.

Beyond single-case reviews, our analytics-driven approach identifies systemic trends — whether documentation gaps, coding patterns, or utilization outliers — and converts findings into prioritized remediation plans. For payers and risk-bearing entities, we design utilization management programs that reduce waste while preserving quality. For providers, our audits uncover opportunities to improve documentation, recapture lost revenue and reduce denials through targeted education.

Engage us for a focused pilot to evaluate claims from a specific period or service line; we’ll return a concise findings report, estimated financial opportunity and recommended next steps. Our goal is to deliver measurable financial recovery, lower future spend, and improved documentation practices that sustain those gains over time.
